Revised Punching Rules at Office Locations:                                     w.e.f. 1st Jan 2021

Noida offices & Pune Office-

1.      Office timings from 8:45 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. including 30 minutes lunch break     (1:15 – 1:45). 

2.     Employees are expected to punch their card every day, while entering or leaving the office premises, unless they are on outdoor duty or tour.  First punch in the day will be treated as IN PUNCH and last punch in the day will be taken as OUT PUNCH.

3.      In case IN PUNCH is between (8:45 am and 9:15 am, employee is required to make up time lost due to late arrival by completing working period of 8 hours & 45 minutes (+ 30 minutes lunch) or else 2nd half will be marked as leave.  For example, if arrival is at 9:10 am he/she will be marked present provided he/she punches out at 6:25 pm (i.e. 6:00 pm + 25 minutes).

4.     In case IN PUNCH is later than 9:15am, first half will be treated as on leave.  However, three late punches upto 10:15am will be allowed in a calendar month provided the employee completes 8 hours & 45 minutes of working time (+30 minutes lunch) on the same day, else half day will be marked as leave.

5.     For first half attendance, employee needs to punch as per 8:45 am to 1:15 pm and second half as per 1:45 pm to 6:00 pm.

 Chennai Office –

1.      Office timings from 9:00 a.m. to 6:15 p.m. including 30 minutes lunch break     (1:15 – 1:45). 

2.     Employees are expected to punch their card every day, while entering or leaving the office premises, unless they are on outdoor duty or tour.  First punch in the day will be treated as IN PUNCH and last punch in the day will be taken as OUT PUNCH.

3.      In case IN PUNCH is between (9:00 am and 9:30 am, employee is required to make up time lost due to late arrival by completing working period of 8 hours & 45 minutes (+ lunch) or else 2nd half will be marked as leave.  For example, if arrival is at 9:25 am he/she will be marked present provided he/she punches out at 6:40 pm (i.e. 6:15 pm + 25 minutes).

4.     In case IN PUNCH is later than 9:30 am, first half will be treated as on leave.  However, three late punches upto 10:30 am will be allowed in a calendar month provided the employee completes 8 hours & 45 minutes of working time (+lunch) on the same day, else half day will be marked as leave.

5.     For first half attendance, employee needs to punch as per 9:00 am to 1:15 pm and second half as per 1:45 pm to 6:15 pm.

2.     As such daily 8 hours 45 minutes of working time (+ lunch) is officially required on any given day.

3.      In order to facilitate employees for availing short leave to attend to urgent situations, short leave upto two times in a month for two hours each either in Morning or Evening with prior sanction of HOD is permitted. However, the concerned employee is required to compensate for the lost time within next 6 working days.

4.     Intervening holidays/weekly off will not be counted towards leave.  For Example, if a person goes on leave for 5 days with Saturday-Sunday intervening, he/she will need to apply for leave for 3 days only.

Leave will be credited to the individual account w. e. f. 1st January each year. 

For newly joined employees, the Leave will be calculated on prorata basis from date of Joining till year end & will be credited on a quarterly basis.

For personnel with less than 15 days available leave balance at the start of the year, the leave will be credited on a quarterly basis.

The accumulated SL as of 31st December 2020 will continue to be maintained in employee’s leave credit separately and those employees who have any credit under accumulated SL will continue to be permitted to avail this leave on sickness purpose till such credit is available. The accumulated Sick Leave can be availed only in the case of an employee falling sick for a longer period and exceeds his entitlement in that particular year. This requires Business Head approval. The employee must submit adequate proof of his illness including a Medical certificate.

To claim tax benefits towards LTC, an employee will need to take minimum 3 days continuous leave.

As per Maternity Act, all eligible women employees are allowed 182 days (26 weeks) Maternity Leave.

Leave & Attendance rules for Contractual staff:-

The Contractual staff will be entitled for 16 days leave per annum (4 days leave every quarter which will get credited on the 1st day of the quarter). Un-availed leaves will be en-cashed after the year end as per the policy.  Intervening holiday or weekly off in between leaves are not counted.
